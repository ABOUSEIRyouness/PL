“On Communicative Competence”
by Dell Hymes (1972) — Summary & Reflection

📘 Introduction

“On Communicative Competence” (1972) is a pivotal article by Dell Hymes—a renowned linguist and anthropologist—that challenges the limited scope of Noam Chomsky’s linguistic competence model. Hymes argues for a broader, socially-aware understanding of language use, one that emphasizes real-life communicative ability over idealized, grammar-focused abstractions.

❌ Critique of Chomsky's Model

Hymes critiques Chomsky’s notion of an ideal “speaker-hearer” in a completely homogeneous speech community, calling it a theoretical construct with little practical relevance. He compares this idealized speaker to a mythical “unicorn,” and contrasts it with real-world learners—like schoolchildren—whose language performance varies widely depending on social and cognitive factors.

He notes that Chomsky’s model focuses solely on an individual’s ability to produce grammatically correct sentences, while neglecting how language is used in everyday communication.

🌍 Language and Social Context

Hymes introduces the concept that language is not just a mental system of rules, but also a social tool shaped by context, relationships, and cultural expectations. Unlike Chomsky’s linguistic competence, Hymes’ model considers how social norms and real-world dynamics impact a person’s ability to use language meaningfully and appropriately.

🔑 The Four Parameters of Communicative Competence

To address these gaps, Hymes introduces four essential parameters of communicative competence:

  • Possibility: Whether a linguistic structure obeys grammatical rules.
  • Feasibility: Whether it’s realistically usable, given memory or cognitive constraints.
  • Appropriateness: Whether it suits the social or cultural context of communication.
  • Actual Occurrence: Whether it reflects what people commonly say or do in real conversation.

These elements broaden the concept of competence from abstract grammar to actual communicative performance in real-life scenarios.

🎓 Implications for Language Teaching

Hymes’ work laid the foundation for modern communicative language teaching (CLT), where language is seen not as a set of grammar rules to memorize, but as a tool for meaningful interaction. Teachers are encouraged to incorporate situational contexts, role-playing, and authentic materials into their lessons—emphasizing how, when, and why language is used rather than just what is grammatically correct.

✅ Conclusion

Dell Hymes’ concept of communicative competence revolutionized linguistic theory and language education by including social, cultural, and practical dimensions of language use. His ideas continue to shape how we teach and learn languages, advocating for a more holistic and realistic understanding of communication.
